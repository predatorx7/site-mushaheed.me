# **That Is So Absurd: The Sponge On Your Head**

Imagine this. You’ve just finished a grueling football match, or maybe you just parked your motorcycle and pulled off your helmet. You look in the mirror expecting to see a sleek, windswept hero, but instead, you are greeted by a chaotic, frizzy dandelion.

You try to run your hands through it to fix it, and *snap, crackle, pop*—strands are breaking, snagging like velcro, and what looks like a snowstorm of dandruff is raining down onto your shoulders.

That is so absurd\! But fear not. You aren't cursed, and your character model isn't glitched. You likely just have extremely porous hair.

### **Is This Patch Note For You? (The Jug Test)**

Before you equip the gear in this guide, you need to verify your hair's stats. Do not rely on the infamous "drop a hair in a glass of water" test—surface tension and microscopic oils will lie to you.

Do this instead: Next time you step into the shower, pour a small jug of water over your dry head.

* **If the water beads up** and rolls off for a few seconds before your hair gets truly wet, you have *Low Porosity*. (This guide is not for you\!)  
* **If your hair instantly soaks it up** and gets completely drenched the microsecond the water hits it, congratulations. You have **High Porosity** hair. Proceed to the next level.

### **The Mechanics of the Sponge**

Normally, hair cuticles lay perfectly flat, like the neatly stacked shingles on a roof. They keep the inside of the hair protected. But highly porous hair? Those cuticles are permanently cranked open like microscopic trapdoors.

Because of this, your hair acts exactly like a dry kitchen sponge. It absorbs water instantly. But here is the tragic plot twist: because those trapdoors are wide open, all that precious moisture evaporates straight out into the unforgiving city air just as fast as it entered. It leaves your hair bone-dry, structurally weak, and impossible to shape.

### **The Hidden Boss: Hard Water**

Before we talk about loot, we need to check your environment. If the water coming out of your shower is "hard water" (packed with harsh minerals like calcium and magnesium), it will completely block your hair from absorbing moisture, making the dryness 10x worse.

**How to test for it:**

1. **The Dry Test:** Look at your bathroom taps and showerhead. Are there crusty, chalky white stains on the metal? That is hard water buildup.  
2. **The Amazon Route:** Buy a simple, cheap "Water Hardness Test Strip" kit online. Dip it in a glass of your shower water and read the color chart.

**The Fix:** If you have hard water, you need to nerf it. You can install a hard water shower filter (like WaterScience or Cleo) directly onto your showerhead. Alternatively, use a jug of filtered drinking water for the final rinse of your hair to wash out those harsh minerals.

* **Scalpe Pro:** Your targeted, anti-fungal weapon. Deploy this strictly on the scalp terrain to clear the dandruff.

### **Crafting Your Defense (The L.C.O. Stack)**

So, how do we equip this gear? You need to use the "Liquid, Cream, Oil" combo to build an impenetrable wall.

Step out of the shower. Your hair is dripping wet (**L**iquid). Do not aggressively rub it with a towel\! Instead, while it is still soaking, take your Cantu Shea Butter (**C**ream) and squish it directly into the wet lengths of your hair. The water helps carry the thick cream deep into the porous gaps.

Immediately follow up with the Promend Serum (**O**il/Sealant). Rub it between your palms and glide it over the surface. You have just slammed those trapdoors shut and locked the hydration inside.

**Survival Tactics: AFK, Travel & The Beach**

Your hair will face environmental hazards outside the bathroom. Here is how you survive them:

**1\. The 20-Day Reset (Haircuts)**

Highly porous hair frays easily. Once a strand splits at the end, that split will travel all the way up to the root, destroying the hair. Listen to your hairstylist: get a trim every 20 days. You only need to chop the microscopic dead ends to keep the health bar full.

**2\. On-The-Go / No Time To Shower**

If you are out running errands, finished a ride, and have zero time to shower, do NOT splash water on your head to fix your hair. Water without sealant just causes frizz. Instead, slightly dampen your palms with a drop of water, add 1 drop of your Promend Serum, rub your hands together, and gently *pat* the outside of your hair to tame the chaos.

**3\. The Beach / Ocean Biome Hazard**

If you find yourself on a tropical shoreline, beware the ocean. Saltwater is catastrophic for porous hair. **Never jump into the ocean with dry hair.** Because your hair is a sponge, it will instantly soak up 100% saltwater, which strips all oil and snaps the strands.

* *The Hack:* Before hitting the waves, drench your hair with fresh tap water and coat it with a layer of Cantu cream. If the "sponge" is already full of good moisture, there is no room for the damaging saltwater to enter\! Wash it out immediately after your swim.

### **The Golden Rule (Warning\!)**

Listen very carefully, because this is where most runs end in a Game Over.

**Never, ever dry-brush highly porous hair.**

When your hair is dry, those open cuticles act like microscopic Velcro hooks. If you try to run your fingers or a comb *through* the strands, they snag on each other, rip, and break. That is where your hair fall is coming from\!

Treat your dry hair like a delicate clay sculpture, not a lawn that needs raking. You only manipulate the shape on the surface.

Armed with your heavy-hitting shea butter, your strategic scalp treatments, and the golden rule of "hands off when dry," you are finally ready to face the world with a perfectly sealed, impenetrable mop of hair.
